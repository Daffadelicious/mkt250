Week 03 | Sourcing the product

By BriAnna Marsh

This week was very insightful for me. Something I've noticed over the years is that customers are starting to become more and more aware of where their purchased products are being produced from. It's interesting to see discussions on things like fast fashion, vegan, or animal cruelty free production is becoming more and more prominent especially in the beauty industry. While I'm personally all for these strides I can see it shutting out new people and ideas they could bring to different industries due to the accelerated cost of production when those extra steps are taken. Joe's group is inexperienced and low on funds and time which was the biggest thing I took into consideration while choosing a manufacturer and deciding between affiliate and drop shipping. With the rate that society is moving towards these more expensive but better sourced products I can't help but feel like this group would have a difficult time getting their business off the ground unless they pour more money into ethically sourced products. It feels like cheaply sourced goods might become a thing of the past here in the future. That aside, I think stepping away from manufacturing and focusing on an affiliate link would be best for their situation. It's a way for them to dip their feet into the pool of an online business without jumping straight into the deep end and biting off more than they can chew. My thought process is that they could learn how to create a proper website and get some good traffic going to it. Then, once they've mastered that they can jump into drop shipping or any other more risky but more profitable business model. Overall, I enjoyed this week of class and I feel like I learned a lot!
